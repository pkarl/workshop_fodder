“Hey Philo, can I ask you something?” May says, setting down her tools.

“Sure,” Philo answers.

“Is it weird to be invented?”

“What do you mean by invented?” Philo says.

“I mean, you get rebuilt all the time. We found you in the desert without a body. You can be updated with new programming. Like just now — we installed Japanese and some stuff about plants and retrieved a bunch of your stored data on the Casino. Isn’t that weird?”

“I suppose it would be ‘weird’ if there were some other option. But this is all I know,” Philo says. “You were invented, in your own way.”

“I wasn’t invented, I was born,” May says.

“Yes, but someone made you. Consider the fact that absolutely no living thing came into existence on its own. That was a decision made by someone else. We are all here involuntarily,” Philo says.

“Well that’s uplifting,” May says.

“It’s a fact,” Philo says.

“So if we all came here against our will, how come we feel like we’re so important?” May asks.

“Maybe it’s because someone thought we were important enough to invent,” Philo says.

“Well I’m glad someone invented you, whoever that was” May says.

“And I’m glad someone invented you, whoever that was,” Philo says, “so you could update my programming.”
